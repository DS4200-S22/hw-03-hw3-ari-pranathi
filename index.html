HTML+CSS & Marks+Channels

This chart shows items and attributes. To be specific the attribute types are categorical, and ordered quantitatively in a sequential manner. Areas are the marks used as items, and the channels include position (both), color, tilt, and area.

Bump chart show years which are attributes ordered quantitatively in a sequential manner. Furthermore, other data types include items, links, categorical and ordinally ordered attributes. Here the marks used are dots and lines, with the dots being used to represent items/nodes, and the lines being used as links to represent connection. Channels for this graph are color, tilt, line lenght, and position (both).

Mind maps show links, qualitative and categorical attributes. These data types are illustrated through the use of areas and lines as links to show connection. Furthermore, mind maps employ the use of color and length.

Stacked bar charts illustrate categorical and sequential quantitative (ordered) data. The lines, which are the marks, are used as items/nodes. On top of that color, area, position (both x and y), and length are employed as the channels.
